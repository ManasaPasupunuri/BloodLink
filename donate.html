The Donation Process

1

Registration

Sign in, complete health history questionnaire, and receive a mini-physical.

2

Health Screening

Quick check of temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels.

3

Donation

The actual donation takes only 8-10 minutes with the entire process lasting about an hour.

4

Refreshments

Enjoy snacks and drinks while resting for 15 minutes before leaving.

Frequently Asked Questions

Whole blood donors can donate every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate up to 24 times per year. Power Red donors can donate every 112 days (16 weeks).

The entire blood donation process takes about one hour, though the actual blood collection only takes about 8-10 minutes. The process includes registration, health history, mini-physical, donation, and refreshments.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment used is sterile and disposed of after a single use. The average adult has about 10 pints of blood, and only 1 pint is taken during a donation.

Get plenty of sleep, eat a healthy meal, drink extra water, and avoid fatty foods before donating. Wear clothing with sleeves that can be raised above the elbow. Bring your donor card, driver's license, or two other forms of identification.

There are several types of donations: whole blood (most common), platelet donation, plasma donation, and Power Red (double red cell) donation. Each type helps different kinds of patients and has different requirements.
