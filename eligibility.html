Basic Eligibility Requirements

Age Requirements

You must be at least 18 years old to donate blood through BloodLink. In some states, 16-17 year olds may donate with parental consent.

Weight Requirements

You must weigh at least 50kg (110 lbs) to be eligible to donate blood. This requirement helps ensure donor safety.

Health Status

You should be in good general health and feeling well on the day of donation. Specific health conditions may affect eligibility.

The Donation Process

1. Registration

You'll need to complete a donor registration form and provide identification.

2. Health History & Mini-Physical

A trained staff member will ask you confidential questions about your health history and check your temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin level.

3. Blood Donation

The actual blood donation typically takes about 8-10 minutes, during which approximately 450ml (one pint) of blood is drawn.

4. Refreshments & Recovery

After donating, you'll be invited to rest and enjoy refreshments for 15-20 minutes before leaving.

5. Post-Donation Care

Drink plenty of fluids, avoid strenuous activities for the rest of the day, and keep the bandage on for several hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (about 8 weeks). If you donate platelets, you can donate more frequently - up to 24 times per year.

Many medications do not prevent you from donating blood. However, the eligibility depends on the medication and the condition being treated. Common medications like blood pressure medicine, birth control pills, and hormone replacement therapy typically don't affect eligibility.

People with diabetes are eligible to donate if their diabetes is well-controlled with diet or oral medication. Those who use insulin may be eligible if their diabetes is stable.

If you got your tattoo or piercing at a state-regulated facility, you can typically donate after 3 months. If the facility was not state-regulated, a 12-month waiting period may be required.

You might feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but the actual donation process should not be painful. Our trained staff works to make the experience as comfortable as possible.
